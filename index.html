Rescaled Water Index (RWI)

A New Spectral Index for Mapping Water Surfaces in Urban Contexts

Eduardo F. Justiniano1, Fernando S. Kawakubo1, Edimilson R. dos Santos Jr.2, Breno M. de Melo2, Gustavo P. Menezes2, Marcel Fantin1, Julio C. Pedrassoli3, Marcos R. Martines2, Rúbia G. Morato1
1University of São Paulo 2Federal University of São Carlos 3Western Michigan University
Paper (PDF) DOI Preprint Code Dataset

Interactive comparison of RWI, MNDWI and NDWI for multiple South‑American cities.

Abstract

The Rescaled Water Index (RWI) is an exponential rescaling of the green band in combination with SWIR1 that suppresses urban shadows and dark impervious materials while enhancing true water pixels. We benchmark RWI against NDWI, MNDWI, AWEIsh/nsh across six South‑American cities using Sentinel‑2 imagery and 123 k reference samples. RWI outperforms competing indices in 3 out of 6 cities and yields the highest mean pROC score overall.
